Understanding Stock Quote

  • Previous Close: The price at which a stock traded on the last trading day. This indicates the closing value of the stock in the previous trading session, providing a reference point for assessing price changes.
  • Open: The price at which a stock began trading on the current trading day. This reflects the initial price level at the beginning of the trading day, influencing early market sentiment.
  • Bid: The highest price a buyer is willing to pay for a stock. This represents the demand side of the market, showing the maximum price buyers are willing to pay.
  • Ask: The lowest price a seller is willing to accept for a stock. This represents the supply side of the market, indicating the minimum price sellers are willing to accept.
  • Day Range: The difference between the lowest and highest prices at which a stock has traded during the current trading day. This illustrates the intraday price volatility and trading range of the stock.
  • 52-week Range: The lowest and highest prices at which a stock has traded during the last 52 weeks (one year). This provides a broader perspective on a stock's performance, indicating its price range over a significant timeframe.
  • Volume: The total number of shares traded during a specific time period, often measured on a daily basis. This reflects market activity and liquidity, with higher volumes suggesting increased investor interest.
  • Avg. Volume: The average number of shares traded daily over a specific time period, typically 50 or 200 days. This offers a smoothed-out view of a stock's trading activity, indicating its typical daily liquidity.
  • Market Cap: The total market value of a company's outstanding shares of stock, calculated by multiplying the current stock price by the number of outstanding shares. This represents the overall value of the company in the stock market.
  • Beta (5Y Monthly): A measure of a stock's volatility in relation to the market, indicating how much the stock price tends to move compared to a benchmark index. This helps assess a stock's risk relative to the broader market.
  • PE Ratio (Price-to-Earnings Ratio): The ratio of a company's share price to its earnings per share (EPS), reflecting the market's expectations for a company's future earnings growth. This indicates the valuation of a stock relative to its earnings.
  • EPS (Earnings Per Share): The portion of a company's profit allocated to each outstanding share of common stock, providing insight into a company's profitability on a per-share basis. This helps evaluate a company's profitability and earnings distribution.
  • Earnings Date: The date on which a company is scheduled to release its quarterly or annual financial results. This key for investors anticipating and reacting to a company's financial performance.
  • Forward Dividend & Yield: The estimated annual dividend payment per share and the dividend yield, expressed as a percentage of the current stock price. This relevant for income-seeking investors, indicating the company's dividend policy and yield.
  • Ex-Dividend Date: The date on or after which a stock is traded without a previously declared dividend or distribution. This determines eligibility for receiving upcoming dividends.
  • 1y Target Est (1-Year Target Estimate): The median target price forecast by analysts covering a particular stock, representing their estimate. This provides an analyst consensus on the stock's expected future price. These metrics collectively offer valuable insights into a stock's past performance, current market dynamics, and analysts' expectations for the future.
